## 2026March Mentor Reflection
March was not designed to teach Roblox alone. It was designed to build the way Sean thinks.

Rather than introducing advanced game mechanics immediately, I intentionally slowed the learning pace and focused on the invisible foundations behind every computer system. During this month, we repeatedly explored object hierarchy, coordinate systems, parameter observation, conditional logic, execution order, and client-server concepts using simple demonstrations before applying them inside Roblox Studio.

Instead of asking Sean to memorize code, I consistently encouraged him to understand why each instruction exists. Whenever he encountered difficulty, we discussed the underlying system rather than the answer itself. As his confidence grew, I gradually shifted from direct instruction to guided questioning, and eventually asked him to explain concepts to his younger brothers. Teaching became part of the learning process, allowing him to verify his own understanding through communication.

By the end of March, I observed an important change. Sean no longer approached programming as isolated commands to remember. He began viewing it as a connected system where different tools could achieve the same objective through logical reasoning. More importantly, he became comfortable asking questions, testing ideas, observing outputs, and refining his own thinking.

Looking back, the greatest achievement of this month was not learning Roblox. It was establishing a systematic learning mindset that will continue to support his future learning, regardless of which technology or programming language he encounters.
